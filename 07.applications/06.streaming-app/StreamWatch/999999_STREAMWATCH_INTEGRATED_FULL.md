## 1. Purpose

This document defines the mission, product positioning, and strategic role of StreamWatch inside StreamingOS.

## 2. Official Definition

StreamWatch is the official viewer-front application of StreamingOS.
It is the primary viewing application used to discover, enter, resume, and continue video assets, live streams, archived streams, clips, series, and playlists.

## 3. Mission

The mission of StreamWatch is to make video and live-stream viewing inside StreamingOS:

- easy to discover
- easy to enter
- easy to continue
- easy to unlock when access is gated
- easy to move across devices and screen sizes

## 4. Positioning

StreamWatch is positioned as:

- the main viewer application of StreamingOS
- the canonical playback-entry surface for streaming content
- a structured discovery application rather than a flat-feed-only viewer
- a continuity-first viewing application rather than a single-session player
- a TV-ready and profile-aware viewer product
- a viewing-context commerce surface that can unlock content without breaking the viewing journey

## 5. Core Differentiation

The core differentiators of StreamWatch are:

### 5.1 Category-tree discovery
Discovery is based on a real tree with branch structure, breadcrumb context, and sibling movement.

### 5.2 Explicit continuity model
History, progress, queue, favorites, watch later, playlists, and entitlement-backed watchability remain separate concepts.

### 5.3 Live and archive unity
Waiting room, active live viewing, archived replay, and clip entry all belong to one viewer journey.

### 5.4 TV-ready playback
Route handoff and large-screen viewing are treated as first-class behavior.

### 5.5 Entitlement-aware watchability
The app understands whether content is playable now, not just whether content exists.

## 6. Relationship to Adjacent Applications

### 6.1 Relationship to StreamStudio
StreamStudio is creator-side.
StreamWatch is viewer-side.

### 6.2 Relationship to Civilization surfaces
Civilization may expose marketplace, promotions, and horizontal product discovery.
StreamWatch provides viewing-native execution and playback entry.

### 6.3 Relationship to shared commerce
StreamWatch is not the canonical billing ledger.
It is a commerce-capable execution surface connected to shared commerce logic.

## 7. Fixed Non-Goals

StreamWatch is not intended to become:

- a creator editing application
- an upload management console
- a publication approval console
- a revenue split control console
- a billing ledger
- a raw file delivery manager

## 8. Final Positioning Statement

StreamWatch is the official StreamingOS viewer-front application that combines structured discovery, entitlement-aware access, profile-based continuity, and TV-ready playback into one coherent viewing product.

## 1. Purpose

This document fixes what StreamWatch includes, what it excludes, and where the application boundary must remain stable.

## 2. In-Scope Responsibilities

StreamWatch includes:

- viewer home and discovery surfaces
- category-tree browsing
- search and result entry
- live watch, archive watch, clip watch, and video watch entry
- library behavior including favorites, watch later, history, playlists, and following
- series detail and continuity surfaces
- profile-aware viewing behavior
- restriction gate UX
- purchase, rental, and membership execution surfaces
- entitlement-aware CTA switching
- TV route handoff and large-screen viewing entry
- comment and report viewer entry points
- follow and notification viewer behavior

## 3. Out-of-Scope Responsibilities

StreamWatch excludes:

- creator editing
- upload intake
- publication approval workflow
- content operations console behavior
- creator collaboration operations
- revenue split administration
- billing ledger ownership
- refund ownership
- direct binary file delivery ownership
- DRM control ownership

## 4. Upstream Dependencies

StreamWatch depends on upstream systems for:

- content preparation
- content publication state
- pricing and catalog setup
- commerce contract setup
- membership product setup
- entitlement grant result
- notification framework availability

## 5. Downstream Effect Zone

StreamWatch drives or influences the following operational outcomes:

- playback entry
- progress capture
- history creation
- follow state changes
- watch-later and favorites mutations
- TV handoff initiation
- viewer comment entry
- viewer report entry
- commerce execution requests
- entitlement refresh after commercial success

## 6. Boundary Rule for Commerce

Purchase, rental, and membership may be executed from StreamWatch.
However, StreamWatch is an execution surface, not a separate commerce truth owner.

## 7. Boundary Rule for Entitlement

Entitlement, playback eligibility, and archive availability remain StreamingOS-canonical even when the commercial flow starts outside StreamWatch.

## 8. Boundary Rule for Continuity
