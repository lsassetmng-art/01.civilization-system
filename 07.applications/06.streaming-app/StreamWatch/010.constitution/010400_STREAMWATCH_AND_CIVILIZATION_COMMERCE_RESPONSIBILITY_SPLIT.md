## 1. Purpose

This document defines how StreamWatch and Civilization surfaces divide responsibility when both can expose purchase, rental, and membership actions.

## 2. Shared Rule

Both StreamWatch and Civilization may act as commercial entry surfaces.

This means both may expose:

- buy now
- rent now
- join membership
- product discovery
- price visibility
- promotion entry

## 3. Civilization-Facing Strengths

Civilization is strong at:

- horizontal discovery
- marketplace surfaces
- cross-domain promotions
- campaign presentation
- non-viewing-context product comparison
- creator/channel exposure before direct watch intent exists

## 4. StreamWatch-Facing Strengths

StreamWatch is strong at:

- viewing-context purchase
- viewing-context rental
- viewing-context membership join
- locked-content CTA presentation
- entitlement refresh immediately before playback
- immediate transition from unlock to watch

## 5. Shared Commerce Backbone

Although both surfaces may execute commercial actions, they must not diverge into separate transaction truth systems.

The shared commerce backbone owns:

- pricing truth
- transaction confirmation
- billing truth
- contract truth
- refund truth
- renewal truth

## 6. Output Split

The result of commercial success is:

- contract truth in commerce
- entitlement grant in StreamingOS
- watchability refresh in StreamWatch

## 7. Boundary Protection

Civilization must not become the direct canonical owner of streaming watchability.
StreamWatch must not become the direct canonical owner of billing and contract truth.

## 8. Final Split Statement

Civilization and StreamWatch may both sell access, but commerce truth remains shared and playback truth remains StreamingOS-owned.
