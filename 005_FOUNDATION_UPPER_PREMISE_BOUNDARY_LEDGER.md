# FOUNDATION UPPER PREMISE BOUNDARY LEDGER

## 1. purpose
This ledger fixes how `00.foundation` should function as the upper premise layer
for the whole `01.civilization-system` root.

Foundation is the highest premise layer.
It provides base principles, worldview, and non-fragmented top-level constraints.

Foundation does not replace domain ownership of each OS.

## 2. foundation core meaning
Foundation owns:
- whole-system premise
- worldview-level base meaning
- top-level design principles
- non-domain-specific core constraints
- upper interpretation rules for the civilization-system root

Foundation does not own:
- domain runtime truth
- PersonaOS canonical domain truth
- BusinessOS canonical domain truth
- LifeOS canonical domain truth
- GameOS canonical domain truth
- StreamingOS canonical domain truth
- application-local product truth
- portal route truth

## 3. control scope principle
Foundation may define:
- what kinds of things the system is
- top-level architectural values
- top-level system boundaries
- irreversible base constraints
- interpretation priorities when conflicts appear

Foundation must not define in excessive detail:
- app-level screen behavior
- domain-internal runtime states
- OS-specific operational records
- low-level implementation details that belong to lower layers

## 4. foundation to root boundary

### 4-1. foundation to CivilizationOS
Foundation may define:
- whole-civilization top-level premise
- systemwide architectural priorities
- upper non-domain-specific principles

Foundation must not replace:
- civilization-os domain-owned common operational truth
- civilization-os controlled shared runtime meanings

### 4-2. foundation to PersonaOS
Foundation may define:
- top-level premise for identity/personhood interpretation only at a universal level
- upper principles that constrain persona architecture

Foundation must not replace:
- persona identity authority
- persona rendering authority
- persona distribution authority
- persona interaction semantics owned by PersonaOS

### 4-3. foundation to BusinessOS
Foundation may define:
- upper principles for responsibility, boundary, and control
- civilization-wide architectural discipline affecting business design

Foundation must not replace:
- business-side operating truth
- business sync/share/send authority
- company-side business interpretation details

### 4-4. foundation to LifeOS
Foundation may define:
- upper principles for life-domain handling at the worldview/architecture level
- non-domain-specific constraints about ownership and responsibility

Foundation must not replace:
- life record ownership
- life support meaning
- household/personal operational meaning

### 4-5. foundation to GameOS
Foundation may define:
- upper worldview constraints for game existence within the civilization-system
- non-game-specific top-level interpretation priorities

Foundation must not replace:
- game rule authority
- game world-state authority
- game progression meaning

### 4-6. foundation to StreamingOS
Foundation may define:
- upper architectural constraints for streaming as a system participant
- top-level principles affecting public systems

Foundation must not replace:
- creator/channel/content authority
- ranking/donation/streaming platform authority
- streaming runtime operational truth

### 4-7. foundation to Applications
Foundation may define:
- top-level product boundary discipline
- high-level system interpretation constraints
- what applications are allowed to be in relation to OS layers

Foundation must not replace:
- app-specific product ownership
- app-local UX decisions
- app-local runtime specialization

### 4-8. foundation to Portal Site
Foundation may define:
- top-level public-entry discipline
- high-level official/public interpretation rules
- upper architectural constraints for official access routes

Foundation must not replace:
- portal route ownership
- portal navigation truth
- portal public exposure operations

## 5. interpretation rule
Foundation decides:
- the top
- the broad
- the irreversible
- the shared premise

Lower layers decide:
- the domain
- the operational
- the concrete
- the runtime

## 6. escalation rule
When a meaning dispute occurs, review in this order:

1. Is this a whole-system premise issue?
2. Is this an OS-domain issue?
3. Is this an application/portal realization issue?

Only send a topic upward to Foundation
when it is truly top-level and cross-domain.

## 7. forbidden patterns
The following are forbidden:

1. using Foundation as a dump for unresolved lower-layer design
2. writing OS runtime details as Foundation truth
3. putting application UX rules into Foundation as if universal
4. using Foundation to bypass canonical OS ownership
5. using Foundation to silently redefine portal/app/domain authority

## 8. authority sentence
Foundation constrains.
Domains define.
Products realize.
