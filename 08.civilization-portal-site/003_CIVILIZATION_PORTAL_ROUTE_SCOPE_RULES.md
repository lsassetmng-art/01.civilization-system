# CIVILIZATION PORTAL ROUTE SCOPE RULES

## 1. purpose
This document fixes route-scope rules for the official portal.

## 2. route types
Portal routes may include:
- domain routes
- app routes
- category routes
- showcase routes
- landing routes
- official cross-links

## 3. route scope boundaries
### domain route
A route pointing to an OS/domain entry area.
It must not pretend to own the destination domain.

### app route
A route pointing to a concrete application.
It must not redefine the application's internal truth.

### category route
A route grouping multiple official destinations.
It must remain navigational, not authoritative.

### showcase route
A route displaying approved public highlights.
It must be derived from owner-approved exposure scope.

### landing route
A route optimized for user entry and orientation.
It may summarize, but must not replace domain truth.

## 4. route composition rule
A portal route may include:
- title
- short summary
- owner label
- destination label
- category
- public status
- entry action

A portal route must not include:
- unauthorized owner claims
- owner-internal-only logic as public truth
- duplicated master definitions that belong to destination domains

## 5. route authority rule
Route authority belongs to portal only for:
- entry
- navigation
- discoverability
- route presentation

Destination authority remains with the destination owner.

## 6. route lifecycle rule
A route should move through:
- draft
- approved
- public
- revised
- retired

Portal should not present draft routes as official public routes.
