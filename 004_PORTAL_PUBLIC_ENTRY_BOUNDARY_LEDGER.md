# PORTAL PUBLIC ENTRY BOUNDARY LEDGER

## 1. purpose
This ledger fixes how the official portal should expose, route, and present
destinations across the civilization-system root.

The portal is the official public entry system.
It does not replace domain truth owners.

## 2. portal core meaning
Portal owns:
- official public entry
- official top-level navigation
- official discoverability
- official route presentation
- official access gateway meaning

Portal does not own:
- PersonaOS canonical truth
- BusinessOS canonical truth
- LifeOS canonical truth
- GameOS canonical truth
- StreamingOS canonical truth
- application-internal product truth

## 3. routing principle
Portal may:
- introduce
- list
- categorize
- guide
- link
- route
- expose official destinations
- present official summaries that are derived from owner-approved meanings

Portal must not:
- redefine owner meanings
- silently extend owner scope
- publish unofficial domain claims as official
- act as the final master for destination domains

## 4. portal to root boundary

### 4-1. portal to CivilizationOS
Portal may expose:
- official civilization entry
- official common routes
- official top-level navigation
- approved public civilization summaries

Portal must not replace:
- civilization-wide common operating meaning
- civilization-wide upper rule ownership

### 4-2. portal to PersonaOS
Portal may expose:
- official persona entry points
- official persona discovery routes
- approved public persona showcases
- approved distribution or marketplace routes

Portal must not replace:
- persona identity authority
- persona rendering authority
- persona distribution authority

### 4-3. portal to BusinessOS
Portal may expose:
- official business app entry
- official business product categories
- official business-facing discovery routes

Portal must not replace:
- business operation truth
- business-side common service truth
- company operational ownership meaning

### 4-4. portal to LifeOS
Portal may expose:
- official life-app entry
- official life support categories
- public-facing life product discovery routes

Portal must not replace:
- life-domain support truth
- life-domain ownership rules
- personal life record authority

### 4-5. portal to GameOS
Portal may expose:
- official game entry
- game discovery routes
- game public summaries
- launch/access routes

Portal must not replace:
- game runtime authority
- game world-state authority
- game rule authority

### 4-6. portal to StreamingOS
Portal may expose:
- official streaming entry
- creator/content/channel discovery routes
- public ranking or showcase routes when owner-approved
- public viewing routes

Portal must not replace:
- streaming platform truth
- creator/channel ownership authority
- ranking/donation authority

### 4-7. portal to Applications
Portal may expose:
- app catalog routes
- app landing entries
- app category pages
- official distribution/discovery entry
- official route to concrete applications

Portal must not replace:
- app-internal product flow ownership
- app-local feature meaning
- app runtime behavior authority

## 5. publishability rule
A destination is portal-publishable only when:
- the destination exists as an approved official route
- the summary shown on portal is owner-approved or owner-derived
- the route does not overstate the owner's scope
- the destination is allowed to be publicly discoverable

A destination is not portal-publishable when:
- it is internal only
- it is draft only
- it is owner-private
- it is local-only
- it lacks governance for public exposure

## 6. public summary rule
Portal summaries must be:
- short
- directional
- non-authoritative beyond portal scope
- clearly derived from destination-owner meaning

Portal summaries must not:
- invent domain truth
- declare implementation detail as official architecture truth
- flatten all destinations into one indistinguishable layer

## 7. authority sentence
Portal routes.
Owners define.

## 8. review checklist
Before adding a new portal route, confirm:

1. Is this an official destination?
2. Is public exposure allowed?
3. Who owns the destination truth?
4. Is the portal text derived from that owner?
5. Is portal acting only as entry and navigation?
