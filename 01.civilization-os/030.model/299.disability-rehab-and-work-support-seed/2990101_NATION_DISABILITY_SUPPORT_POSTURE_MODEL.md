# ============================================================
# 1. HELIOS
# ============================================================

posture:
- no strong independent large-scale welfare facility baseline
- no independent disability support residence baseline
- no broad public disability support institution baseline
- household, class, and employer-linked support remains stronger than civic welfare support
- industrial rehabilitation and work-return support are only marginally realistic
- weak citizens are easily left outside stable support
- this remains a legacy of disposable-labor era logic and old noble parliamentary dominance


# ============================================================
# 2. NOVA
# ============================================================

posture:
- urban institutional disability support exists
- private and premium support models are strong
- rehabilitation, assisted daily life, and work-return support are service-structured
- class difference in support quality remains visible


# ============================================================
# 3. SEIWA
# ============================================================

posture:
- public disability support is strong
- rehabilitation, education support, and work support are institutionally integrated
- civic accessibility and structured assistance are recognized


# ============================================================
# 4. GLADIA
# ============================================================

posture:
- institutional support exists
- military injury, service rehabilitation, and structured return support are strong
- discipline and controlled support environments are common


# ============================================================
# 5. ORPHEUS
# ============================================================

posture:
- family and island-community support are primary
- small local support rooms and practical assistance are more natural than large institutions
- island accessibility and household support are central


# ============================================================
# 6. AURELIA
# ============================================================

posture:
- state-managed support is strong
- disabled persons may be assigned supervised roles within state or doctrinal structure
- protection and control coexist
- long-term care is state responsibility when recognized


# ============================================================
# 7. FREE CITIES UNION
# ============================================================

posture:
- city-specific variation is high
- some cities use public institutional support
- some cities rely on household and community support
- mixed civic, guild, and neighborhood models coexist


# ============================================================
# 8. FINAL RULE
# ============================================================

Disability support posture must align with eldercare posture
and broader family-state support structure.
