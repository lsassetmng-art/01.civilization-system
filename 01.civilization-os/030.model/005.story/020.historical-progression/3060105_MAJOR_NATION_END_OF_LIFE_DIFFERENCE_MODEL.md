# 1. HELIOS
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- class strongly shapes funeral quality, inheritance stability, and business continuity
- upper households preserve continuity; weak households are less protected


# ============================================================
# 2. NOVA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- asset value, estate management, and business control dominate post-death transition
- wealth continuity is highly visible


# ============================================================
# 3. SEIWA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- procedural order and legal stability dominate death-to-inheritance flow
- family continuity is institutionally buffered


# ============================================================
# 4. GLADIA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- service honor and household support shape memorial and succession logic
- death can reinforce state recognition and family stabilization


# ============================================================
# 5. ORPHEUS
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- grief, ritual, kinship, and island community strongly shape end-of-life handling
- succession remains relational and community-embedded


# ============================================================
# 6. AURELIA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- doctrine and instability distort memorial and succession order
- state power can overrun private continuity


# ============================================================
# 7. FREE CITIES UNION
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- city-by-city variation and small-business succession are strongest
- family, guild, and civic settlement all remain visible


# ============================================================
# 8. FINAL RULE
# ============================================================

End-of-life difference must remain strong enough
to shape mourning, inheritance, and continuity outcomes.
