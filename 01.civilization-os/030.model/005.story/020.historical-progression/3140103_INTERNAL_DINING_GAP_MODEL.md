# ============================================================
# 1. HELIOS
# ============================================================

internal_gap_notes:
- upper districts maintain ceremonial and prestige dining
- worker districts favor filling and efficient meals
- outer districts show survival-oriented food pressure


# ============================================================
# 2. NOVA
# ============================================================

internal_gap_notes:
- executive districts consume premium and display-oriented food
- ordinary dense districts depend on fast outside meals and commercial convenience
- nightlife districts have distinct alcohol-heavy food culture


# ============================================================
# 3. SEIWA
# ============================================================

internal_gap_notes:
- gap exists, but broad access to stable everyday food reduces extreme divergence
- school, rail, and family meal systems soften class-based food instability


# ============================================================
# 4. GLADIA
# ============================================================

internal_gap_notes:
- service-linked households have more stable practical food access
- detached marginal households experience more limited variety


# ============================================================
# 5. ORPHEUS
# ============================================================

internal_gap_notes:
- island variation matters more than formal class difference alone
- brewing and fish-rich islands differ from smaller subsistence islands


# ============================================================
# 6. AURELIA
# ============================================================

internal_gap_notes:
- protected pockets and fragile districts eat very differently
- cheap stalls and state-linked supply dominate where stability is weak


# ============================================================
# 7. FREE CITIES UNION
# ============================================================

internal_gap_notes:
- port cities, guild cities, and civic-rich cities have distinct food ecologies
- local markets shape everyday food more than federation identity


# ============================================================
# 8. FINAL RULE
# ============================================================

Internal dining gaps must remain visible
so that national averages do not erase lived food difference.
