# ============================================================
# 1. HELIOS
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- class and inherited property strongly affect marriage and housing timing
- lower strata may enter work early but stabilize family life later
- upper strata may form households through family-backed property and succession


# ============================================================
# 2. NOVA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- marriage and childbirth are often delayed by career and city cost
- strong earners stabilize faster than ordinary urban workers
- rental-first adulthood is common


# ============================================================
# 3. SEIWA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- the most orderly and institutionally buffered household formation path
- marriage, childbirth, and housing are comparatively predictable


# ============================================================
# 4. GLADIA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- compulsory service creates a major timing distortion
- post-service reentry is a major adulthood branching point
- supported service families form a distinct stable route


# ============================================================
# 5. ORPHEUS
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- kinship and island households strongly shape marriage, childbirth, and residence
- community-supported adulthood is more visible than formal independent timing


# ============================================================
# 6. AURELIA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- unstable labor and housing often destabilize family timing
- reconstruction conditions make adulthood fragile


# ============================================================
# 7. FREE CITIES UNION
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- household timing differs heavily by city and local economic culture
- there is no single federation-wide adulthood script


# ============================================================
# 8. FINAL RULE
# ============================================================

Marriage, childbirth, and housing timing must visibly differ
to support nation-specific family life patterns.
