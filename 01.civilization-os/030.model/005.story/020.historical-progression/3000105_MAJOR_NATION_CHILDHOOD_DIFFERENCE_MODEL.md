# ============================================================
# 1. HELIOS
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- childhood inequality is structurally inherited
- upper districts convert child potential into status more easily
- weak households face unstable support and weaker institutional buffering


# ============================================================
# 2. NOVA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- childhood is highly sensitive to income, housing density, and family work style
- skill orientation begins earlier than in more civic states


# ============================================================
# 3. SEIWA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- childhood is the most institutionally supported
- school readiness and public developmental support are strongest


# ============================================================
# 4. GLADIA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- childhood is shaped by service logic, discipline, and structured family support


# ============================================================
# 5. ORPHEUS
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- childhood is shaped by kinship, island life, culture, music, ritual, and craft proximity


# ============================================================
# 6. AURELIA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- childhood is unstable, state-shaped, and ideologically filtered


# ============================================================
# 7. FREE CITIES UNION
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- childhood support differs heavily by city, district, and civic capacity


# ============================================================
# 8. FINAL RULE
# ============================================================

These differences must remain stable enough
to drive later education and life-course events.
