# ============================================================
# 1. HELIOS
# ============================================================

timing_posture:
- first stable work begins relatively early for lower and middle strata
- upper strata may enter family business through inheritance timing rather than open competition
- marriage timing is class-sensitive
- housing access is strongly tied to class position and family assets
- outer households may delay stable family formation because support is weak


# ============================================================
# 2. NOVA
# ============================================================

timing_posture:
- first income generation is valued earlier than formal status completion
- marriage may be delayed by career competition and urban cost
- childbirth timing is strongly affected by housing cost and work style
- rental life is extended in dense urban zones
- high earners may form premium households faster


# ============================================================
# 3. SEIWA
# ============================================================

timing_posture:
- orderly transition from schooling to work
- relatively stable marriage and household formation timing
- childbirth and family formation are supported by structured institutions
- housing acquisition is gradual and practical
- legal, public, and stable employment routes support predictable adulthood


# ============================================================
# 4. GLADIA
# ============================================================

timing_posture:
- life course is strongly affected by compulsory service between ages 25 and 28
- marriage and childbirth may be delayed or rescheduled around service
- return-to-civilian timing shapes later household formation
- service-family households receive structured support
- officer-track continuation creates a distinct adult pathway


# ============================================================
# 5. ORPHEUS
# ============================================================

timing_posture:
- adulthood is more family-and-community linked than institutionally staged
- family business, island work, arts, fishery, brewing, and non-regular labor remain common
- marriage and childbirth timing are shaped by kinship and local support
- ownership and residence can be household-linked rather than market-driven


# ============================================================
# 6. AURELIA
# ============================================================

timing_posture:
- instability, day labor, and reconstruction distort normal adulthood sequencing
- marriage and childbirth may be delayed or made unstable by economic insecurity
- housing is fragile and work continuity is weak
- state-linked roles may partially stabilize otherwise unstable life course


# ============================================================
# 7. FREE CITIES UNION
# ============================================================

timing_posture:
- adulthood timing varies by city, district, and small-business ecology
- some cities resemble stable civic timing
- some resemble workshop, referral, or precarious timing
- household formation is highly local and mixed


# ============================================================
# 8. FINAL RULE
# ============================================================

Life-course timing must remain nation-specific
so that adulthood feels socially differentiated.
