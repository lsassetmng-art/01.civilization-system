# ============================================================
# 1. HELIOS
# ============================================================

posture:
- class structure strongly affects early childhood trajectory
- outer poor and historically weak households face unstable support
- noble and upper households receive better developmental access
- democratization added some child support and outer compulsory access,
  but inequality remains visible from early life
- AI-related trait observation may begin earlier than formal job assignment culture would suggest


# ============================================================
# 2. NOVA
# ============================================================

posture:
- dense urban family support and childcare routing are important
- dual-income and high-rise household conditions strongly affect childhood
- development is quickly linked to school readiness and future skill paths
- family resources strongly shape service quality


# ============================================================
# 3. SEIWA
# ============================================================

posture:
- strongest formal early-childhood support
- nursery, kindergarten, household support, and educational preparation are well connected
- childhood is institutionally protected and strongly routed toward education
- family burden is recognized but mitigated through structured support


# ============================================================
# 4. GLADIA
# ============================================================

posture:
- military-family and service-household conditions shape early childhood
- discipline, fitness, and order may appear earlier in development culture
- formal state-linked child support exists, especially for service families


# ============================================================
# 5. ORPHEUS
# ============================================================

posture:
- household and kinship support dominate early childhood
- island community and family culture shape development strongly
- cultural and ritual participation may begin early
- institutional routing is lighter than family-based care


# ============================================================
# 6. AURELIA
# ============================================================

posture:
- early life is strongly influenced by doctrine and state oversight
- unstable reconstruction conditions may distort childhood quality
- child development may be filtered through ideological or state-protective logic


# ============================================================
# 7. FREE CITIES UNION
# ============================================================

posture:
- childhood support varies by city
- some cities resemble civic institutional support models
- some cities rely on family and neighborhood support
- childhood trajectories are highly city-dependent


# ============================================================
# 8. FINAL RULE
# ============================================================

Early-life event posture must clearly differ by nation
so that childhood conditions are socially legible.
