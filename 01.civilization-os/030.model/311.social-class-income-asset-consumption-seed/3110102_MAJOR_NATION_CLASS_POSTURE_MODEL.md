# ============================================================
# 1. HELIOS
# ============================================================

dominant_structure:
- old_noble_and_inherited_owner_class_remains_visible
- strong upper_vs_outer split
- working_and_fragile_classes remain large
- excluded and weakly protected layers remain socially legible

income_structure_tendency:
- upper income concentration strong
- lower and unstable income tiers visible in outer districts

asset_structure_tendency:
- dynastic and inherited assets strongly concentrated in old upper households
- outer districts have weak asset formation

consumption_structure_tendency:
- upper layers maintain premium and symbolic consumption
- outer layers are often limited to constrained or survival consumption

mobility_posture:
- low

notes:
- Helios preserves one of the strongest class-memory systems in Civilization


# ============================================================
# 2. NOVA
# ============================================================

dominant_structure:
- strong elite commercial and financial class
- large urban aspirational middle
- pressured ordinary urban class
- visible lower service and fragile renter class

income_structure_tendency:
- very strong top-end income concentration
- wide spread between elite earners and ordinary urban workers

asset_structure_tendency:
- wealth concentration is extreme
- asset access is strongly market-mediated

consumption_structure_tendency:
- luxury and premium consumption highly visible
- ordinary households often remain in constrained mass consumption despite urban income

mobility_posture:
- medium

notes:
- upward movement exists through skill and earning performance,
  but market pressure filters who can hold gains


# ============================================================
# 3. SEIWA
# ============================================================

dominant_structure:
- broad stable middle
- visible professional and public-service upper-middle
- relatively smaller fragile bottom
- class differentiation exists but is moderated

income_structure_tendency:
- middle-heavy

asset_structure_tendency:
- modest property and stable household assets are more common
- dynastic concentration is weaker than Helios and Nova

consumption_structure_tendency:
- stable mass consumption is dominant
- luxury consumption is present but less socially overwhelming

mobility_posture:
- medium

notes:
- Seiwa has the most institutionally stabilized class structure


# ============================================================
# 4. GLADIA
# ============================================================

dominant_structure:
- service-linked stable households are prominent
- command, veteran, and military-industrial layers are visible
- fragile detached households exist but are less dominant than in Helios or Aurelia

income_structure_tendency:
- medium with service-linked stabilization

asset_structure_tendency:
- property accumulation exists, but military and service structure shapes access
- elite command pathways exist but are narrower than Helios noble continuity

consumption_structure_tendency:
- practical and service-linked consumption dominates
- premium consumption exists but is less central than in Nova

mobility_posture:
- medium

notes:
- service connection is a major class stabilizer


# ============================================================
# 5. ORPHEUS
# ============================================================

dominant_structure:
- kinship-linked household layers matter more than formal class ladders alone
- small owner, craft, fishery, brewing, and family-business households are visible
- unstable non-regular layers are common but not always socially excluded in the same way as in urban states

income_structure_tendency:
- medium to unstable

asset_structure_tendency:
- modest household assets, boats, local shops, and craft assets matter more than large formal property concentration

consumption_structure_tendency:
- stable modest and household-shared consumption dominate
- luxury display is relatively weak

mobility_posture:
- low

notes:
- island social embeddedness modifies how poverty and status are experienced


# ============================================================
# 6. AURELIA
# ============================================================

dominant_structure:
- unstable fragile and low-income layers are large
- state-linked protected pockets may still exist
- broad stable middle is weak
- excluded or disrupted households are highly visible

income_structure_tendency:
- low and unstable

asset_structure_tendency:
- weak household asset continuity
- reconstruction and instability erode property continuity

consumption_structure_tendency:
- survival and disrupted consumption dominate

mobility_posture:
- very_low

notes:
- class instability is one of Aurelia's core social signatures


# ============================================================
# 7. FREE CITIES UNION
# ============================================================

dominant_structure:
- mixed workshop, guild, merchant, civic, and precarious urban layers coexist
- no single federation-wide dominant class ladder
- city ecology matters more than broad federal average

income_structure_tendency:
- medium with sharp local variation

asset_structure_tendency:
- small ownership and workshop control are socially important
- large concentration exists in some cities but is not universal

consumption_structure_tendency:
- stable mass, constrained mass, and small premium pockets coexist

mobility_posture:
- medium

notes:
- local networks and workshop access strongly affect lived class position


# ============================================================
# 8. FINAL RULE
# ============================================================

Major nation class posture must remain clearly differentiated
so that class experience feels socially specific.
