# ============================================================
# 1. HELIOS
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- risk is absorbed unequally by class
- weak households can fall quickly after labor loss, injury, or illness
- institutional rescue is limited


# ============================================================
# 2. NOVA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- economic collapse is more visible than total social collapse
- debt, failure, and performance loss strongly shape adult risk


# ============================================================
# 3. SEIWA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- structured systems reduce chaos
- crisis still hurts, but recovery channels are more legible and procedural


# ============================================================
# 4. GLADIA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- service-linked risk is strongest
- injury, service interruption, and reentry timing matter more than in other states


# ============================================================
# 5. ORPHEUS
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- sea, weather, and household burden are major risk channels
- kinship often absorbs shock better than institutions


# ============================================================
# 6. AURELIA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- crisis cascades rapidly because labor, housing, and state continuity are unstable


# ============================================================
# 7. FREE CITIES UNION
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- risk experience depends strongly on city ecology, guild support, and neighborhood resilience


# ============================================================
# 8. FINAL RULE
# ============================================================

Risk differences must visibly affect
life stability, class reproduction, and household survival.
