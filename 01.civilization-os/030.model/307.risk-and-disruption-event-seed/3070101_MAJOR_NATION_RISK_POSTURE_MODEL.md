# ============================================================
# 1. HELIOS
# ============================================================

risk_posture:
- lower and outer households are highly vulnerable to long-term disruption
- weak welfare buffering makes illness, injury, and job loss structurally dangerous
- class position heavily affects recovery speed
- industrial accidents and labor displacement are important risk channels


# ============================================================
# 2. NOVA
# ============================================================

risk_posture:
- urban competition, housing cost, and income volatility create strong economic fragility
- business failure, debt stress, and career collapse are highly visible risks
- strong earners recover faster, weak earners fall faster


# ============================================================
# 3. SEIWA
# ============================================================

risk_posture:
- strongest institutional buffering
- illness, job loss, and family disruption are more likely to be absorbed through structured support
- legal and civic recovery channels are comparatively strong


# ============================================================
# 4. GLADIA
# ============================================================

risk_posture:
- service injury, training injury, and post-service transition risk are highly visible
- military discipline reduces some public disorder risks while intensifying service-linked risks
- family stabilization after service disruption is institutionally important


# ============================================================
# 5. ORPHEUS
# ============================================================

risk_posture:
- household and kinship resilience are strong
- island isolation, sea accidents, and weather-linked disruption are highly visible
- formal institutional recovery is weaker than community recovery


# ============================================================
# 6. AURELIA
# ============================================================

risk_posture:
- instability, day labor, weak continuity, and reconstruction fragility create high disruption risk
- illness, injury, loss of work, and housing collapse can cascade quickly
- state intervention may exist but is uneven and politically filtered


# ============================================================
# 7. FREE CITIES UNION
# ============================================================

risk_posture:
- city-specific variation is high
- some cities absorb disruption well through civic structure
- others leave households exposed through small-business fragility and informal labor dependence


# ============================================================
# 8. FINAL RULE
# ============================================================

Risk posture must remain strongly differentiated
so that crisis experience feels nation-specific.
