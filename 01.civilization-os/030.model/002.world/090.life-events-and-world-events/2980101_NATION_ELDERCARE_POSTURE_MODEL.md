# ============================================================
# 1. HELIOS
# ============================================================

posture:
- no independent eldercare facility baseline
- no dedicated retirement residence baseline
- no independent terminal-care facility baseline
- old-age survival remains tied to household, class position, and private means
- weak citizens are not strongly protected by institutional welfare
- this posture remains as a legacy of disposable-labor era values
- old noble interests continue to dominate parliament and block strong welfare institutionalization


# ============================================================
# 2. NOVA
# ============================================================

posture:
- eldercare facilities exist
- urban and class-differentiated senior support is strong
- premium eldercare and commercial long-term care models are common
- dense city eldercare and end-of-life services are formalized


# ============================================================
# 3. SEIWA
# ============================================================

posture:
- eldercare facilities exist
- public and civic elder support is strong
- retirement support, care support, and end-of-life support are institutionally organized
- family support remains important, but facility support is widely recognized


# ============================================================
# 4. GLADIA
# ============================================================

posture:
- eldercare facilities exist
- veteran-linked and service-linked aging support is strong
- structured retirement and controlled care environments are common
- end-of-life support is disciplined and institutionally managed


# ============================================================
# 5. ORPHEUS
# ============================================================

posture:
- family-care dominant
- children are expected to care for parents
- island and kinship networks are primary eldercare mechanisms
- independent eldercare facilities are not the baseline model
- local support may exist, but household care is primary


# ============================================================
# 6. AURELIA
# ============================================================

posture:
- elder citizens are employed as teachers and transmitters of doctrine or knowledge
- the state takes responsibility for their care until the end of life
- eldercare is tied to state protection, ideological continuity, and supervised support
- retirement is not treated as simple withdrawal from social function


# ============================================================
# 7. FREE CITIES UNION
# ============================================================

posture:
- city-specific variation is high
- some cities adopt central-continent-style eldercare facilities
- some cities adopt Orpheus-like family-care expectations
- mixed civic, household, and community support patterns coexist


# ============================================================
# 8. FINAL RULE
# ============================================================

National eldercare posture must remain clearly differentiated
so that aging culture is legible by nation and city.
