# ============================================================
# 1. HELIOS
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- lower and middle strata often enter aptitude-filtered employment routes
- Helios-linked firms frequently use AI trait judgment in hiring for old slave, poor, and commoner strata
- upper strata more often enter inherited business succession regardless of aptitude
- technical and industrial prestige routes remain strong
- class and ownership strongly affect final branch quality


# ============================================================
# 2. NOVA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- direct earning potential matters strongly after graduation
- business, commerce, economics, sales, negotiation, and market skill routes dominate
- skill and money-making performance outweigh pure credential prestige
- ASIC route remains highly technical and selective
- practical success can outrank formal educational pedigree


# ============================================================
# 3. SEIWA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- soft academic society with stable institutional branching
- law, administration, education, rail, engineering, and public-service routes are highly legible
- exam repeat exists but is not the only value system
- academic background matters, but is judged with qualifications, interview, and stability


# ============================================================
# 4. GLADIA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- school-to-work transition is shaped by compulsory service between ages 25 and 28
- civilian path may pause, delay, or reroute because of service
- high performers in service may choose officer-track continuation
- return-to-civilian retraining creates a distinct post-service branch


# ============================================================
# 5. ORPHEUS
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- non-regular work is common
- many routes enter family business, island craft, fishery, brewing, arts, or maritime work
- personal trust and social familiarity shape hiring strongly
- formal credential ladders are weaker than relationship and community routes


# ============================================================
# 6. AURELIA
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- unstable reconstruction and daily-hire work distort normal branching
- interviews and prior work history matter strongly
- civil-engineering reconstruction, manual work, and state-directed roles are common
- academic continuity is frequently broken by instability


# ============================================================
# 7. FREE CITIES UNION
# ============================================================

difference_summary:
- city-specific and small-business branching logic dominates
- workshop, guild, micro-firm, and local referral routes are strong
- there is no single prestige ladder for the entire federation
- transition depends heavily on city, district, and personal connection


# ============================================================
# 8. FINAL RULE
# ============================================================

Post-graduation branching differences must remain strong enough
to shape class reproduction, household timing, and adult identity.
