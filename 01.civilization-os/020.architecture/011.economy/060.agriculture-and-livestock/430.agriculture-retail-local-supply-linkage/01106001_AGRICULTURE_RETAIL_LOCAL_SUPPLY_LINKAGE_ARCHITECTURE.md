# ============================================================
# 1. PURPOSE
# ============================================================

Defines the canonical architecture
for linking agriculture,
small livestock,
egg production,
dairy production,
honey production,
fish production,
and preserved food production
to local retail facilities.

This architecture defines:

- producer objects
- local supply routes
- self-consumption and surplus sale separation
- retail intake of local products
- local producer contracts
- food resilience contribution


# ============================================================
# 2. CORE PRINCIPLE
# ============================================================

Agriculture and small-scale production
must not exist as isolated decorative systems.

They must connect to:

- household self-sufficiency
- local sale
- neighborhood exchange
- retail stocking
- daily life essentials continuity


# ============================================================
# 3. SUPPLY PRINCIPLE
# ============================================================

Producer output may flow to:

- household self-consumption
- direct local sale
- cooperative collection
- retail facility contracts
- district market distribution

Retail facilities must be able to accept
local producer supply as a formal route.


# ============================================================
# 4. ECONOMIC PRINCIPLE
# ============================================================

Local supply linkage may improve:

- freshness
- local support
- margin stability
- shortage resistance
- self-sufficiency score
- essential goods access

Local supply may also create
micro-business opportunities.


# ============================================================
# 5. FINAL RULE
# ============================================================

Agriculture-retail linkage
must function as a structural bridge
between production, daily life, and local economy.
