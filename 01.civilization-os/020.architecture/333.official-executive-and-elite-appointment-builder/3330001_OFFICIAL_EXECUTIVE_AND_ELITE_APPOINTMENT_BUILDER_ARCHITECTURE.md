# ============================================================
# 1. PURPOSE
# ============================================================

Defines the canonical architecture
for appointment, promotion, confirmation,
retention, removal, and succession
of senior officials, executives, commanders,
religious elites, and quasi-public leadership
in Civilization seed-world planning.


# ============================================================
# 2. CORE PRINCIPLE
# ============================================================

Appointment systems must distinguish between:

- formal appointment authority
- eligibility and qualification rules
- practical influence over selection
- promotion pipeline
- removal and purge logic
- succession continuity

A role may be formally merit-based
but practically patronage-based.
A role may be formally appointed
but effectively reserved for a class,
faction, military lineage, business elite,
or doctrinal circle.


# ============================================================
# 3. REQUIRED SUBSYSTEMS
# ============================================================

The builder must include:

- Office Category Builder
- Appointment Rule Builder
- Promotion Pipeline Builder
- Confirmation Builder
- Removal and Discipline Builder
- Succession Continuity Builder
- Influence and Capture Builder
- Validation Engine


# ============================================================
# 4. OFFICE COVERAGE
# ============================================================

The builder must support at least:

- senior bureaucracy
- state enterprise executives
- railway and port executives
- military high command
- police and internal security command
- education leadership
- public health leadership
- religious institution leadership
- reconstruction authority leadership
- city and local executive appointments


# ============================================================
# 5. FINAL RULE
# ============================================================

Appointment design must always show
who is formally appointed,
who is practically selectable,
who can block entry,
and who can survive removal.
