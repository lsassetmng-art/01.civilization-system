# ============================================================
# 1. PURPOSE
# ============================================================

Defines the canonical architecture
for nation-level and city-level social statistics
used to represent demographic stability,
family formation,
labor fragility,
poverty,
and social inequality
in Civilization seed-world planning.


# ============================================================
# 2. CORE RULE
# ============================================================

Social statistics must reflect:
- nation identity
- welfare posture
- education posture
- labor-market posture
- household formation timing
- marriage and childbirth timing
- instability and risk structure

These are not abstract metrics only.
They must remain interpretable through the world model.


# ============================================================
# 3. REQUIRED DIMENSIONS
# ============================================================

Each nation seed should define:
- birth and fertility posture
- marriage and divorce posture
- employment and unemployment posture
- poverty and household fragility posture
- disability and elder burden posture
- inequality posture
- city variation if relevant


# ============================================================
# 4. FINAL RULE
# ============================================================

Social statistics seed must make visible
how societies differ in stability,
family formation,
risk exposure,
and class reproduction.
