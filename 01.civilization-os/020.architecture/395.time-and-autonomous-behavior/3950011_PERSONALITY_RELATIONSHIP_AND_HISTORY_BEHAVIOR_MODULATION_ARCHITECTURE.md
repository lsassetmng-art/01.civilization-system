# ============================================================
# 1. PURPOSE
# ============================================================

Define the canonical architecture
for behavior modulation
by personality,
traits,
life environment,
relationships,
and accumulated history
inside Civilization.


# ============================================================
# 2. CORE PRINCIPLE
# ============================================================

Autonomous behavior
must not depend only on time and schedule.

Behavior must also be modulated by:

- personality
- traits
- life environment
- household context
- social relationships
- prior history
- current mental and social posture


# ============================================================
# 3. MODULATION FAMILIES
# ============================================================

Representative modulation families include:

- temperament and personality
- risk preference
- sociality
- aggression or restraint posture
- family structure
- wealth and deprivation background
- work and school history
- prior illness or injury
- prior incident involvement
- friendship and organizational linkage
- prior success or failure experience


# ============================================================
# 4. BEHAVIOR IMPACT
# ============================================================

These modulation factors may affect:

- routine life activity
- political and social participation
- abnormal behavior risk
- recruitment response
- event participation
- purchase behavior
- recovery and avoidance choices


# ============================================================
# 5. FINAL RULE
# ============================================================

Behavior modulation architecture must remain:

- actor-specific
- history-aware
- relationship-aware
- environment-aware
- not purely schedule-generic
